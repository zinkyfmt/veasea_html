Clinical Development

The goal of the Verséa Rx botanical clinical program is to gain IND approval with multiple product candidates. Following IND approval each botanical candidate will undergo pre-clinical toxicologic assessments followed by human trials where Verséa will seeking to be advance one of the first hemp-derived botanical extract-based drug product candidates at the FDA.

Research & Development

Verséa is evaluating new drug delivery approaches, molecules and applications of ethical pharmaceuticals to address unmet medical needs. Our medical hemp research is focused on studying components of the hemp plant individually and in combinations to assess in-vitro and in-vivo response across multiple body systems. Physiologic response determines the therapeutic target for each candidate. This process allows Verséa to build a pipeline of New Chemical Entities and Botanical Rx drug candidates. Areas of interest to Verséa for ethical drug and botanical drug and product development are:

  • Pain
  • Inflammation
  • Anxiety and Mood Disorders
  • Disorders of the Central Nervous System
  • Cancer
  • Infectious Disease

Breeding Program

The Verséa breeding program is designed to translate lab-created new chemical entities to botanical chemovars that are replicated and scaled for cultivation. The process can also be used in reverse to understand how a chemovar already cultivated may affect the body. Innovative techniques and processes will allow for the rapid scale up to produce drug substance and product for clinical trials through the Rx botanical program.

Clinical Development

Our clinical program is aimed at advancing ethical and Rx botanical candidates towards drug approvals. The initial focus of Verséa is on the Rx botanical clinical program. The near-term goal of this program is to submit for and gain approval of INDs with multiple hemp-derived Rx botanical candidates. Verséa is positioned to a first mover in this market segment.

Portfolio of development candidates will have the necessary quality studies performed positioning them for advanced clinical development.
